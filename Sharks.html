Helicoprion

This unique mouth form allowed the fish to crush its prey's shell.

October 30, 2025 Belén López

Most of the information obtained of this animal is theoretical only, there has not been any evidence confirming its whole physiology or its common prey. However, due to the discoverments of its teeth, some experts assume that this shark used to eat Amonites and used its saw-shaped teeth to crush their shell and eat them.

These prehistoric fish, like any other modern fish, had skeletons made of cartilage, making it difficult to determine what Helicoprions looked like, because the skeleton is not complete. Almost all Helicoprion specimens are fossilised 'tooth whorls' that look like spiraled teeth, resembling a saw or a lollipop.

Megalodon

This popular fish gained popularity mainly because of its massive volume and its capacity to attack everything that moved.

October 30, 2025 Belén López

The Megalodon was considered the biggest shark that ever existed, with 14 to 24 meters on average of its body length. It ate from prehistoric turtles to large whales if it was on its range and it had a bite force of up to 108,500 to 182,200 newtons which allowed it to puncture its prey's organs until it was dead. To put this into perspective, the strongest known bites coming from modern animals are the saltwater crocodile at around 16,414 newtons, while a great white shark's bite is estimated at a maximum of about 18,000 newtons. 

Its large size may have been due to climatic factors and the abundance of large preys, and it may have also been by the evolution of regional endothermy (warm-blooded animals) which would have increased its metabolic rate and swimming speed.
